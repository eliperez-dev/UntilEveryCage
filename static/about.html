About Until Every Cage is Empty

Their power is secrecy. Our power is information.

What Is This Project?

Until Every Cage is Empty is an interactive map that exposes the infrastructure of animal exploitation across the world. Using publicly available government data, we've compiled and mapped tens of thousands of facilities including slaughterhouses, processing plants, animal research laboratories, commercial breeders, dealers, and exhibitors.

This mapping tool brings together data from multiple federal agencies (USDA in the U.S., BVL in Germany, FSA in the UK) into a single, searchable interface that allows users to discover animal exploitation operating in their own communities and across the nation.

Our Motivation

We created this tool because transparency drives change. The animal exploitation industry deliberately operates out of public view. By making this concealed infrastructure visible and accessible, we aim to:

  • Shatter Ignorance: Help people confront the true scale of animal exploitation
  • Fuel Investigation: Provide researchers, journalists, and advocates with data to expose wrongdoing
  • Power Resistance: Give activists precise information to organize campaigns
  • Force Accountability: Make it impossible for facilities to hide violations from public scrutiny

Why This Matters

Billions of animals are processed through the facilities mapped on this site every year. These are sentient beings with the capacity for pain and suffering, yet their exploitation happens behind walls designed to keep you from witnessing it. By mapping where this violence occurs, we believe we can:

  • Strip away the industry's veil of secrecy and force public reckoning
  • Arm consumers and policymakers with uncomfortable truths
  • Connect local communities with hidden exploitation operating in their backyards
  • Challenge an industry that profits from public ignorance

Our Commitment

This project is committed to accuracy, transparency, and relentless accountability. We use only publicly available government data and encourage users to approach their advocacy work through legal, strategic channels. Our goal is not simply to document the problem, but to provide the tools necessary to dismantle it.
